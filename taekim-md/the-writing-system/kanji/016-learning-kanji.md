#### 16 Learning Kanji

All the resources you need to begin learning Kanji are on the web for free. You can use dictionaries online such as [Jim Breen's WWWJDIC](http://nihongo.monash.edu/cgi-bin/wwwjdic?1C) or [jisho.org](http://jisho.org/). They both have

great Kanji dictionaries and stroke order diagrams for most Kanji. Especially for those who are just starting to learn, you will want to repeatedly write

out each Kanji to memorize the stroke order. Another important skill is learning how to balance the character so that certain parts are not too big or

small. So make sure to copy the characters as close to the original as possible. Eventually, you will naturally develop a sense of the stroke order for

certain types of characters allowing you to bypass the drilling stage. All the Kanji used in this guide can be easily looked up by copying and pasting to an

online dictionary.
