#### 23 Defining grammatical functions with particles

We want to now make good use of what we learned in the last lesson by associating a noun with another noun. This is done with something called

particles. Particles are one or more Hiragana characters that attach to the end of a word to define the grammatical function of that word in the

sentence. Using the correct particles is very important because the meaning of a sentence can completely change just by changing the particles. For

example, the sentence "Eat fish." can become "The fish eats." simply by changing one particle.
